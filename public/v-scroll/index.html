Elytra Filament Pavilion

Victoria and Albert Museum, London

Inspired by beetles \\ fabricated by robots

As a centrepiece of the V&A’s Engineering Season it demonstrates how architectural design can unfold from a synergy of structural engineering, environmental engineering and production engineering, resulting in unique spatial and aesthetic qualities.

Instead of being merely a static display, the pavilion constitutes a dynamic space and an evolving structure. The cellular canopy grows from an onsite fabrication nucleus, and it does so in response to patterns of inhabitation of the garden over time, driven by real time sensing data.

We invite you to explore the movement trends and the outdoor comfort maps, gain insight start experiencing the structure in a whole new way.
